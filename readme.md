# 3-Month Body Recomposition Plan

> Target: 76kg → 70kg | Duration: 12 Weeks | Training Days: 5/Week

A structured training and nutrition program designed for sustainable fat loss and lean muscle development. This plan combines dumbbell resistance training, incline treadmill conditioning, and swimming to maximise caloric expenditure while preserving muscle mass.

---

## 1. Weekly Training Schedule (5 On / 2 Off)

A balanced split that prioritises recovery between sessions to prevent overtraining and reduce injury risk.

| Day | Session | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Rest | Active recovery — 5k-8k steps |
| Tue | Combo A | 20min Dumbbells + 10min Treadmill (12% Incline) |
| Wed | Swim | 30-40min Breaststroke — focus on long glides, steady pace |
| Thu | Combo B | 20min Dumbbells + 10min Treadmill (12% Incline) |
| Fri | Rest | Active recovery — 5k-8k steps |
| Sat | Combo A | 20min Dumbbells + 10min Treadmill (12% Incline) |
| Sun | Swim | 30-40min Breaststroke — increase lap speed progressively |

---

## 2. Dumbbell Circuit (20 Minutes)

Perform all 5 exercises consecutively as one round. Rest 60 seconds between rounds. Complete 3-4 rounds per session.

**1. Goblet Squats — 12 Reps**
Hold one dumbbell at chest height. Lower until thighs are parallel to the floor. Maintain an upright torso throughout the movement.

<img src="images/global_squat.jpeg" width="60%">

**2. Dumbbell Lunges — 10 Reps per Leg**
Hold dumbbells at your sides. Step forward until both knees reach 90 degrees. Keep chest upright and avoid leaning forward.

<img src="images/dumbbell-static-lunge.webp" width="100%">

**3. Overhead Press — 10 Reps**
Press dumbbells from shoulder height to full lockout overhead. Engage the glutes to stabilise the lower back throughout the movement.

<img src="images/dumbbell-standing-overhead-press.webp" width="60%">

**4. Dumbbell Rows — 12 Reps**
Hinge forward at 45 degrees with a flat back. Pull dumbbells towards the hips, retracting the shoulder blades at the top of each rep.

<img src="images/bentoverrow_dumbbell_full_large.png" width="60%">

**5. Weighted Plank — 45 Seconds**
Hold a standard elbow plank position with a weight plate or small dumbbell placed on the lower back. Maintain a straight line from head to heels.

<img src="images/plank.jpeg" width="60%">

---

## 3. Treadmill Conditioning (10 Minutes)

Perform immediately after the dumbbell circuit to maximise fat oxidation.

* **Incline:** Fixed at 12%
* **Speed:** 3.0 mph (brisk walk)
* **Key Rule:** No holding the handrails. Arm swing engages the core and increases caloric output.

---

## 4. Nutrition Plan

A high-protein, carb-controlled approach to support fat loss while maintaining training performance.

* **Pre-Workout (Fasted):** Water or black coffee only
* **Breakfast (Post-Workout):** 200g Greek Yogurt + 250ml HL Milk + 2 Eggs + 15g Almonds + 2 Oat Bread + 3 Fruits
* **Lunch / Dinner:** 1 serving Protein + 2 servings Vegetables + 0.5 serving Rice
* **Rest Day Adjustment (Mon/Fri):** Reduce to 1 Oat Bread. Remove rice from lunch and dinner.

---

## 5. Progressive Overload Schedule

Increase training intensity every 4 weeks to ensure continued adaptation and avoid plateaus.

| Phase | Dumbbell Weight | Treadmill Speed | Plank |
|---|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | 8-10kg (baseline) | 3.0 mph | Bodyweight or 2.5kg |
| Month 2 | +2kg on all exercises | 3.2-3.4 mph | +5kg plate |
| Month 3 | +2kg additional | 3.5 mph or 1min slow run | Hold for 60 seconds |

* **Month 1 — Foundation:** Establish proper form and mind-muscle connection on every exercise.
* **Month 2 — Strength:** Increase load. The final 2-3 reps of each set should feel challenging.
* **Month 3 — Intensity:** Reduce rest periods, increase treadmill speed, and push through the final fat loss phase.

---

## Success Metrics

* **Weight:** Target loss of ~0.5kg per week (6kg total over 12 weeks)
* **Appearance:** Broader shoulders, flatter midsection — driven by high protein intake and compound pressing/rowing movements
* **Performance:** Noticeable improvement in cardiovascular endurance during daily activities and swimming
